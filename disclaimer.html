1. Nature of Data

⚠️ Key Statement

All reports published by N City Research provide experimental, heuristic proxies for online sentiment and social activity. These results are not validated measurements of real-world happiness, wellbeing, or quality of life.

The indexes produced by N City Research are exploratory and experimental in nature. They are derived from publicly available online content and use proprietary signal detection methods that serve as indirect proxies only. No claim is made that these indexes directly or accurately measure the happiness, satisfaction, mental health, or quality of life of any individual or population.

5. Scope & Representativeness

📊 Key Statement

Online data is self-selected and may not represent the demographics or views of the total population of the cities analysed.

All data is collected from publicly visible online community forums. The following scope limitations apply:

  • Online forum users do not represent the general population of any city
  • Participation is voluntary and self-selected, introducing significant selection bias
  • Residents who do not use online forums — including older adults, non-English speakers, and those without internet access — are not represented
  • Each report analyses a snapshot of recent posts at a given point in time and may not reflect longer-term community sentiment
  • Data is not weighted or adjusted for city population or demographic differences
  • The proprietary methodology behind the indexes is not fully disclosed

N City Research does not intend to defame, disparage, harm, or negatively characterise any city, municipality, community, resident group, or individual. 7. Social Media Data & Third-Party Terms

Data is collected exclusively from publicly accessible online content in a manner consistent with the terms of use of the relevant platforms. No private data, login-protected content, or personal user information is collected or published. All data is analysed at an aggregated, anonymised level. No individual user is identified, targeted, or profiled.

N City Research is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to any social media platform or online service from which data is collected.

8. Suggested Use

N City Research reports are best used as:

  • One informal reference point among many when thinking about community wellbeing
  • A conversation starter about how digital sentiment might reflect community mood
  • A supplementary, informal data point alongside official wellbeing surveys
  • A longitudinal trend indicator — changes over time are more meaningful than absolute values

Reports should not be used as a standalone measure of city happiness, a basis for policy decisions, real estate investment decisions, or any other consequential judgment about a city or its residents.
