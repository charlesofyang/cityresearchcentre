About

What we do

We collect and analyse publicly available social media data to produce two independent indexes tracking how Ontario communities express themselves online over time.

📡

Data Collection

We gather publicly available posts from online community forums on a quarterly basis, analysing digital sentiment signals across city communities.

📊

Independent Indexes

Two separate, clearly defined indexes — Happiness and Vitality — are produced independently and never combined into a single score.

🗓️

Quarterly Reports

Reports are published quarterly, allowing trends to be tracked over time. Each report is freely available to download and share.

🔓

Free & Open

All reports are published free of charge. This is a non-commercial, independent research initiative with no advertising or funding.

Our Indexes

Two independent measures

We deliberately keep our two indexes separate, as they measure fundamentally different aspects of community life.

😊
Happiness Index
Measures the frequency of positive sentiment signals in public online posts from city communities. A higher score reflects more frequent expression of positive emotion in digital communication. This is our primary index and the core focus of this research.
🎉
Vitality Index
Measures the frequency of social gathering and community activity signals in public online posts. A higher score reflects a more socially active and outwardly engaged community. This index measures activeness, not happiness — the two are intentionally kept separate.
